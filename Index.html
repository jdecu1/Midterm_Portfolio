About

Albert Einstein transformed modern physics.

He recast space and time as a single fabric, linked mass to energy, and showed how light can free electrons.

This work launched relativity and helped begin the march toward quantum theory.

Important Discoveries

  • Photoelectric Effect and Light Quanta
  • Brownian Motion Explanation
  • Mass–Energy Equivalence (E=mc²)
  • Gravitational Redshift Prediction
  • Light Bending by Gravity
  • Stimulated Emission Concept

Theories

  • Special Relativity (1905): Unifies space and time and holds speed of light constant in all inertial frames, leading to time dilation, length contraction, and E=mc².

  • General Relativity (1915): Treats gravity as curvature of spacetime produced by mass–energy, explaining orbital precession and predicting gravitational waves and black holes.

  • Einstein’s A and B Coefficients: Defines absorption, spontaneous emission, and stimulated emission—the basis for lasers and masers.
