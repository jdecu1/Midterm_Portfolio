About

Albert Einstein was a theoretical physicist whose work reshaped our understanding of space, time, energy, and matter. His thinking led to breakthroughs including special and general relativity, the photoelectric effect explanation, and foundational insights that helped launch quantum theory.

Hobbies

  • Playing the violin and piano
  • Sailing
  • Philosophy and writing
  • Long walks and nature
  • Reading literature

Important Discoveries

  • Photoelectric Effect and Light Quanta
  • Brownian Motion Explanation
  • Mass–Energy Equivalence (E=mc²)
  • Gravitational Redshift Prediction
  • Light Bending by Gravity
  • Stimulated Emission Concept

Theories

  • Special Relativity (1905): Space and time form a single entity, spacetime; the laws of physics are invariant in all inertial frames; the speed of light is constant.

  • General Relativity (1915): Gravity is the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy; predicts phenomena such as gravitational waves and black holes.

  • Einstein’s A and B Coefficients: Described absorption, spontaneous emission, and stimulated emission, laying groundwork for lasers.
